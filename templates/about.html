About College Bound Tours

It all began when I took my high school junior on a college tour during spring break — and because we’re a family of five, her younger sisters (a 9th grader and a 5th grader) came along for the ride. Somewhere along the way, my junior fell in love with one of the schools. As we talked through the experience together, a question kept coming up among all three girls — one that stuck with me long after the trip: why doesn't every student have the opportunity to experience this pivotal step in their educational journey?

Why College Visits Matter

Nearly 80% of students report that an on-campus visit significantly influences their decision to apply or enroll. (BHD&P Research)

And according to a 2017 Gallup-Strada poll, approximately half of U.S. adults who pursued or completed a degree would change at least one education decision — their major, institution, or degree type. Those with some college but no degree were the most likely to express regret. A guided college tour could help students make more informed choices and potentially reduce future regret. (Gallup Education Report)

According to Encoura's 2023 State of Campus Visits Report, while 82% of underclass high school students intend to visit college campuses, only 48% of seniors follow through. This missed opportunity has a measurable impact: students who visit a college campus are significantly more likely to enroll. Guided tours can be the deciding factor in turning college aspirations into reality. (Encoura Report)

Introducing the GAM Promise

The GAM Promise is our commitment to helping as many students as possible experience life-changing college visits — regardless of financial status. Named after my daughters — Gabrielle, Adriana, and Monica — the GAM Promise Foundation ensures that 10% of College Bound Tours’ profits go toward funding need-based and sponsored seats for students across our region.

Whether it’s your child’s first trip to a university or a chance to help them feel confident in their college decision, our mission is clear: no student should miss out due to cost.
